The Quiet Room

Secret Door

There is a door behind your sternum
that only opens when you're not looking.
I've been waiting there for hours,
patient as a prayer,
watching the lock turn silver in your sleep.

If you opened it,
what would spill out?
Months of almosts, maybe.
The weight of every word I never said
hanging in the air like snow
that refuses to fall.

I think I know the combination now:
your middle name backwards,
the number of times you've almost left,
the exact shade of blue
your sorrow wears on Sundays.

Altars of Salt

I am building altars from salt
and the small deaths you leave behind:
coffee grounds in the sink,
your name half-erased from my phone,
the way you say "maybe tomorrow"
like a prayer I'm not allowed to answer.

What do I sacrifice here?
Every morning I don't text you first.
Every night I don't dream your face
into the space beside me.

The salt dissolves when it rains.
I rebuild it anyway,
grain by grain,
until my hands are raw
and my mouth tastes like the ocean
where all the drowned prayers go.

Guillotine of Sugar

You are made of sugar and lightning,
sharp enough to cut clean through
but sweet enough that I keep
putting my neck on the block.

Every "good morning" is a small execution.
Every "sleep well" drops the blade.
I collect the pieces of myself
in mason jars, label them
by date and intensity of ache.

When you smile,
the crowd cheers.
When you leave,
they sweep up what's left of me
and throw it to the birds.

Tomorrow I'll climb the scaffold again,
adjust the blindfold,
and wait for you to say my name
like it's the last word
I'll ever need to hear.

Library of Ash

Every book I've loved has burned.
Not in flames, but in the quiet way
that words turn to dust
when you read them too many times,
when you press your thumb
against the same sentence
until the ink bleeds through your skin.

I am walking through the ruins now,
kicking at the remnants
of stories I once believed
could save me from myself.

Here: the ashes of every love poem
I copied into margins.
There: the spine of a philosophy book
that taught me how to think
but not how to stop.

The librarian is long gone.
I am alone with the catalog
of everything I've lost to language,
writing my name in the dust
with a finger that remembers
how to turn pages
that no longer exist.

Cartography of Time

I am mapping the places where time breaks:
3:17 AM when your text lights up my phone,
the moment between waking and remembering
that you're not here,
the second before you say goodbye
and the hour after you're gone.

Here be dragons:
the kitchen where we fought
about nothing and everything,
the bookstore aisle where you first
told me you loved me,
the parking lot where we learned
that some things can't be taken back.

I draw latitude and longitude
around the shape of your absence,
mark each coordinate
where memory bends
like light around a star.

Somewhere in this geography
there's a place where all our almosts
live together,
where the roads we didn't take
converge in a city
I'll never be able to visit.

Winter's Not a Season

Winter's not a season,
it's a state of mind
I've been living in
since you stopped
calling my name
like it meant coming home.

The snow inside my chest
doesn't melt in spring.
It just gets older,
harder,
until I'm walking around
with a glacier for a heart
and frostbite in my fingertips
every time I reach for the phone.

Sometimes I wonder
if you feel the cold too,
if your breath fogs
when you say someone else's name
with the same tenderness
you used to save for mine.

But I think you're living
in a different climate now,
where the sun actually sets
and rises again,
where winter is just
something that happens
and then ends.

Cathedral of Ruin

I built a cathedral from my ribs
and lit every vein like a candle.
The saints turned their faces,
the angels split their wings in shame,
but I prayed anyway ‚Äî
not to heaven, not to mercy ‚Äî
only to the shape of your shadow
etched across my skull.

If you enter me,
the bells will shatter themselves to ringing.
If you leave me,
let the pews drown in ash.
I will crawl through fire like a supplicant,
I will name you in smoke
until the air itself becomes unbreathable.

The Hunger Psalm

Your absence is a famine,
a hollow that eats bone.
I starve on your silence,
gnawing the marrow of memory
until my mouth tastes only rust.

What god made me this way ‚Äî
a beast chained to your footsteps?
If you turned once, just once,
I would break every chain,
devour the sky itself,
swallow the stars like seeds
just to plant your name in my blood.

Elegy with Your Name in It

Bury me, but not in soil.
Bury me in the syllables of your name.
Carve it into my coffin lid,
let worms read it as scripture.

The mourners will not understand.
They will whisper of madness,
of blasphemy, of ruin.
But when I claw from the grave,
when my mouth is dirt and thunder,
it will not be God I cry for.
It will be you. Always you.
